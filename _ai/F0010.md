# F0010 — Commercial Concepts & Website Copy Extraction

## Spec Definition

### Objective

Extract from `README.md` all commercial concepts and produce ready-to-use textual material to flesh out the commercial website for AIAD (AI Assisted Development). The deliverable must be marketing-oriented content, not code.

### Scope

- Source document: `README.md` (root of the repository).
- Target audience: enterprise decision-makers, CTOs, engineering directors, and architects evaluating AI-assisted modernization solutions.
- Tone: professional, reassuring, engineering-oriented, non-hype.
- Output: structured textual content suitable for direct integration into a commercial website (landing page, sections, CTAs, etc.).

### Deliverables

The output must include:

1. **Brand positioning statement** — a one-sentence definition of AIAD and what it stands for.
2. **Tagline / hero slogan** — a short, punchy headline for the homepage hero section.
3. **Hero subtitle** — one or two sentences expanding the tagline.
4. **Value propositions** — a list of 4 to 6 short value statements aimed at attracting customers.
5. **Pain points addressed** — a list of the customer problems AIAD solves, phrased from the customer's perspective.
6. **Key concepts / pillars** — the core principles of AIAD, each summarized in marketing language (not engineering jargon).
7. **Differentiators** — what makes AIAD different from "vibe coding", full rewrites, or unmanaged AI usage.
8. **Target customer profile** — short description of who AIAD is designed for.
9. **Vision statement** — a forward-looking message about the future of software development with AIAD.
10. **Suggested website sections** — a proposed structure (section titles + 1-2 sentence content per section) that a web designer can directly use.
11. **Call-to-action suggestions** — 2 to 4 CTA sentences to drive engagement (contact, demo, discovery call, etc.).
12. **Trust elements** — extracted credibility points (e.g., senior engineering experience, real-world origin of the methodology).

### Constraints

- Preserve the engineering seriousness and anti-hype tone of the README.
- Keep sentences short, clear and customer-focused.
- Each concept must be tied back to something present or implied in `README.md`.
- Output must be in English, matching the README.

### Acceptance Criteria

- All commercial concepts present in the README are captured.
- Content is reusable as-is by a web designer / marketing team.
- No technical implementation is produced.
- Structure is clear, with sections and headings ready for direct mapping to website pages or blocks.
